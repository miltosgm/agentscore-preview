The "TripAdvisor for Real Estate" Problem

Some people think: "I'll just check TripAdvisor for agent reviews like I do for hotels."

Here's why that doesn't work:

1. Wrong Audience

TripAdvisor users are tourists looking for vacation tips. They're not property buyers leaving detailed reviews about agent negotiation skills or developer quality.

2. No Verification

On TripAdvisor, anyone can review anything. On AgentScore, reviewers must verify they worked with the agent. Big difference for a ‚Ç¨200K+ decision.

3. Generic Reviews

TripAdvisor reviews: "Great service, very helpful!" AgentScore reviews: "Negotiated 7% below asking, found hidden issues in inspection, still responds 2 years after purchase."

4. No Professional Incentive

Hotels care about TripAdvisor because tourists use it. Real estate agents don't prioritize it because serious buyers don't look there.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I find real estate agents on TripAdvisor?

Technically some agents may have business listings, but TripAdvisor isn't designed for this. You'll find minimal information and very few (if any) relevant reviews.

Are TripAdvisor reviews reliable for agents?

No. TripAdvisor doesn't verify that reviewers actually worked with the agent or bought property. AgentScore verifies all reviews are from real clients.

Why not just use Google reviews then?

Google reviews are better than TripAdvisor for agents, but still generic. AgentScore focuses specifically on real estate expertise, track record, and post-sale support. See our comparison.

Do agents care about their TripAdvisor listing?

Not really. Serious real estate professionals focus on platforms where buyers actually search (AgentScore, Google, industry directories), not tourist review sites.

Is AgentScore only for buyers, or investors too?

Both! Our agent profiles show specialties including investment properties, rental yield expertise, and market knowledge - crucial for investors.
