Trust Before Transactions

Ads show you properties. AgentScore shows you who to trust.

The Problem with Relying on Ads Alone

Every day, hundreds of real estate ads appear on Google, Facebook, and Instagram showing stunning properties across Cyprus. Beautiful photos, compelling offers, contact forms ready to fill.

But here's what the ads don't tell you:

  • Who actually owns the listing?
  • Is the agent legitimate and trustworthy?
  • What do past clients say about their experience?
  • Does the agent have proper licensing and credentials?
  • Are there any red flags or complaints?

Real Story: The €10,000 Lesson

Elena saw a Facebook ad for a stunning apartment in Limassol. Perfect location, great price. She clicked, filled out the form, and within an hour an agent called.

The agent was pushy. "Three other buyers are interested," he said. "You need to move fast." Elena paid a €10,000 deposit to "secure the property."

Two weeks later: the agent stopped answering calls. The property didn't exist. Elena's €10,000 was gone.

The ad looked professional. The website looked real. But Elena never verified the agent.

The Smart Approach: Use Both, But in the Right Order

Real estate ads aren't bad — they're just incomplete. Here's the smarter way to use them:

The "Trust-First" Property Search Method

  1. Start with AgentScore — Find 2-3 trusted agents in your target area
  2. Check their reviews — Look for agents with 4.5+ ratings and experience in your property type
  3. Contact the agents directly — Ask about listings matching your criteria
  4. NOW browse ads — When you see interesting properties, verify the agent first on AgentScore
  5. Never commit without verification — If an agent isn't on AgentScore or has bad reviews, proceed with extreme caution

Why Ads Don't Show Agent Quality

Real estate advertising platforms (Google, Facebook, Instagram) are designed to sell ad space, not verify credibility. This means:

  • Anyone can advertise — No background checks or licensing verification required
  • The biggest spenders appear first — Ad rank = budget, not trustworthiness
  • No review enforcement — Fake testimonials are common
  • Scammers exploit the system — Professional-looking ads can hide fraudulent operators
  • You're the product — Advertisers pay to capture your contact info, not protect you

When to Trust an Ad (and When Not To)

Ads you can trust MORE:

  • From established agencies with physical offices you can visit
  • Featuring agents with 5+ years of visible track record
  • Backed by recognizable brands (Century 21, Sotheby's, etc.)
  • Including full agent names and licensing info

Red flags to watch for in ads:

  • "Limited time offer" pressure tactics
  • No agent name or credentials visible
  • Prices significantly below market rate
  • "Contact us immediately" urgency language
  • Generic contact forms with no office address
  • Stock photos instead of real agent photos

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I still browse properties on Facebook and Google?

Absolutely! Just verify the agent behind the ad on AgentScore before making contact. Think of ads as property discovery, and AgentScore as agent verification.

What if the agent advertising a property isn't on AgentScore?

That's a yellow flag. Ask them directly: "Can I see your reviews on AgentScore?" If they're not listed, request licensing credentials and check with the Cyprus Real Estate Agents Registration Council.

Do agents pay to be on AgentScore?

No. AgentScore is a public platform where anyone can review real estate professionals. Agents cannot pay to remove bad reviews or boost their rankings artificially.

How do I know AgentScore reviews are real?

We verify reviewer identity and cross-check transaction records where possible. Unlike ads, where testimonials can be fabricated, AgentScore reviews come from real buyers and sellers.

Should I work with multiple agents at once?

It's common in Cyprus to work with 2-3 agents simultaneously. Just be transparent about it. AgentScore makes it easy to compare agents' specializations and reviews to find the right fit.
